# Ecommerce--Olist-Store-Analysis
Purpose:
To provide a comprehensive overview of sales, customer behavior, and logistics performance across the Olist e-commerce marketplace.
Goal:
To help management monitor high-level KPIs ($16.01M Sales, 96.48K Orders, $2.42M Profit), track regional merchant hubs, optimize payment method offerings, and balance product sales against shipping/freight costs.
Key Insights:
Credit Cards Dominate Payments: * Insight: 77K customers use credit cards, dwarfs alternative methods like Boleto (22K) or Vouchers (7K).
Impact: Seamless checkout experiences, fraud prevention, and credit card rewards/partnerships should be prioritized to keep the majority of customers happy.

Weekday vs. Weekend Activity:
Insight: Weekdays drive 77.21% ($11.91M) of sales, with order volumes steadily peaking on Monday (15.70K) and tapering off into Saturday (10.56K).
Impact: Customer support, server capacity, and targeted marketing campaigns should be heavily resourced early in the week (Monday–Wednesday) to maximize conversions when traffic is highest.

Sellers Concentrated in Major Cities:
Insight: Sao Paulo (2.11K) and Rio de Janeiro (1.69K) are the absolute heavyweights for seller density.
Impact: Logistics hubs, fulfillment centers, and merchant acquisition strategies should remain focused on these major Brazilian metropolitan areas to minimize supply chain delays.

Freight Cost vs. Sales Value Strain:
Insight: While Health & Beauty leads total sales contribution at 30.03%, items like Bed, Bath, Table carry disproportionately high freight costs (25.47% freight vs. 27.48% sales). On the flip side, Watches & Gifts is highly efficient (12.51% freight vs. 27.78% sales).
Impact: Heavy/bulky categories (like Bed & Bath) are eating into profit margins through shipping costs. Olist should optimize shipping partnerships or adjust shipping subsidies for bulky categories to protect the bottom line.
